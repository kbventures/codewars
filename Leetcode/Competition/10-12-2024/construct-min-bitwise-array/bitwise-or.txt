Bitwise OR is an operation that compares two numbers bit by bit and returns a new number. For each pair of bits, it outputs 1 if at least one of the bits is 1, and 0 if both bits are 0.

Example:
Let's take two numbers:

A = 5 (in binary: 0101)
B = 3 (in binary: 0011)
Bitwise OR operation:

markdown
Copy code
   0101
OR 0011
--------
   0111  (which is 7 in decimal)
Summary:
A | B (5 OR 3) results in 7.
